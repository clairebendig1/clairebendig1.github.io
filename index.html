About

I've always been passionate about forms of communication: the sentence that gets a developer unstuck at midnight, the style guide that outlasts its author, the system prompt that shapes how an AI speaks to millions of users.

I've built documentation ecosystems across developer platforms, AI infrastructure, and regulated healthcare environments, most extensively at Meta's Reality Labs, where I grew the contributor base from 4 to 56, drove quality compliance from 0% to 91%, and wrote a style guide that three years later became the automated standard for all of Meta's external documentation teams.

More recently I've been working at the intersection of language and AI, designing system prompts and agent behaviors that became the most-used AI tools company-wide, defining frameworks for measuring AI-generated content quality, and coordinating documentation for Meta's Model Context Protocol implementation.

I'll always be driven to explore how language shapes the user's experience, and how to build the taxonomies and standards that make it consistent at scale.
